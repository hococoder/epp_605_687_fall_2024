Josh Steele



Josh works at the Applied Physics Laboratory. He’s a software engineer in the Space Exploration Sector, and has been there since 2002.

He recently capped off over 10 years of work on the MESSENGER project to Mercury where he helped develop the mission’s planning software (SciBox) in Java, and spent the last few years of the mission leading the team that developed that software. He continues to develop scientific analysis applications in Java, and works as a section supervisor and project manager at the Lab.

He also, thanks to the Education and Public Outreach requirements of our NASA space programs, got to flex his iOS programming muscle by developing and maintaining iOS applications for both MESSENGER and New Horizons. He continues to work on mobile applications for Space and our National Health Mission Area.

When not working, Josh has performed in the Columbia Pro Cantare as a Tenor, has coached little league baseball in the Howard County Parks and Rec’s Columbia Baseball League, is a major fan of SciFi (Doctor Who, Star Trek, Star Wars, the list goes on and on) and the Marvel Cinematic Universe and roots on the Hokies and the Washington Commands in football.

Josh also develops teaching material at kodeco.com in addition to teaching iOS development in the JHU Engineering Program for Professionals - you could read about the course here
