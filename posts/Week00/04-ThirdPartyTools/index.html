Class Apple developer account

If you have an Apple ID, you already have access to a free developer account. All you have to do is sign into Xcode’s account dialog with those credentials, and Xcode will setup the required certificates you’ll need to develop. If you don’t have an Apple ID already, you can get one at appleid.apple.com. In this class we’ll be taking it one step further. I have signed up for the iOS Developer University program, which allows everyone in the class to be part of a development team, allowing you to share projects and resource. To help with this, please email rsteele3@jhu.edu with your Apple ID and I will add you to the team!

Setting up an account on JIRA

One of your assignments this semester will be the completion of an App Development Log, which will be used to define the scope of your final project later in the semester. We will be using an instance of the Atlassian suite of tools that I have setup at the URL below. You will need to make an account here and use the issue tracker, JIRA, to capture the requirements and design of your app. So please do the following:

  • Go to jira.hococoder.com in your browser
  • Click on the "Sign up for an account" link on the right hand side
  • Sign up!
  • When you do so, let me know, and I'll setup a project for you and assign you and me to the team

Version Control

I highly recommend version control for your projects. Xcode natively supports Git, and will even setup a local git repo for you when creating a project. An external repositories you use should be marked private. Bitbucket is free for private accounts, and Github is free with an .edu address. If I find that your repo is marked Public, I’ll fail you for that assignment. I am more than happy to take a look at code in your repository if you need help – my user email at both sites is hococoder@gmail.com.
