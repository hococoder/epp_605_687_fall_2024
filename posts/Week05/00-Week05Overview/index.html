Welcome to Week 5! This week we'll delve into some pretty important frameworks in iOS if you want to know what the device is doing in terms of motion or location.

  • Onboard Sensors: Your iOS device is jam packed with sensors - here you'll find out which ones you can use in the simulator, and which ones you need a real device for, and more importantly, what you can do with those sensors
  • Introduction to Core Motion: Information from the built in gyroscope and accelerometer can be retreived from the Core Motion API, giving you detailed information about what orientation your device is in, and how it is moving around in 3D space
  • Introduction to Core Location: Information from the built in GPS and acessing wireless networks can be used to help determine your location on the planet and sometimes, even within buildings
  • Introduction to MapKit: Chances are you've used Maps to find your way around with your device, but did you know that you can embed maps into your own app? MapKit shows you how
  • Programming Assignment 5: Where does everyone live in the class? You'll use MapKit to figure that out!

There is NOT a Week5_Starter or Week5_Final project for this week; example code can be found in the EPExampleApp project in the resource bundle. There is, as usual, a starter project for the homework
